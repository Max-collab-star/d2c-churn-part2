# Part 2: High-Priority Manual Review Cases Log

This evaluation log isolates 10 critical operational customer profiles from the `segments.csv` matrix that demonstrate high anomaly profiles. These edge cases require human intervention and manual customer support operations rather than automated lifecycle email triggers.

---

### Case 1: High-Value Support Escalation Loop
* **Customer ID:** VIP-2081
* **Metrics:** Recency: 4 days, Frequency: 48 orders, Monetary: $3,210, Complaints: 14 tickets
* **Risk Profile:** High-spending brand advocate trapped in a critical operational failure loop. While recent transaction indicators look strong, 14 tickets in a brief window signal imminent churn due to systemic friction.
* **Action Strategy:** Executive phone intervention within 24 hours. Issue a direct refund credit alongside dedicated support ownership.

### Case 2: Post-Complaint Silence (Sudden Drop)
* **Customer ID:** CHR-9921
* **Metrics:** Recency: 58 days, Frequency: 12 orders, Monetary: $840, Complaints: 5 tickets
* **Risk Profile:** Past advocate who completely stopped purchasing immediately following a cluster of 5 unresolved customer service issues. 
* **Action Strategy:** Priority outbound service recovery email containing an explicit apology from the operations manager and a high-value unconditional friction-remediation voucher.

### Case 3: High-Monetary Low-Frequency Anomalous Spike
* **Customer ID:** WHL-4032
* **Metrics:** Recency: 88 days, Frequency: 1 order, Monetary: $1,450, Complaints: 0 tickets
* **Risk Profile:** Wholesale or bulk enterprise purchaser who placed a single massive order and has neared the churn boundary line without re-engaging.
* **Action Strategy:** Dedicated B2B sales outreach to offer corporate account tier structures, volume pricing incentives, and personalized order configurations.

### Case 4: High-Frequency Low-Value Ticket Generator
* **Customer ID:** TCK-1104
* **Metrics:** Recency: 12 days, Frequency: 22 orders, Monetary: $110, Complaints: 9 tickets
* **Risk Profile:** Low-margin consumer utilizing disproportionate support bandwidth. Highly active transaction history but extremely low average order value coupled with repetitive complaints.
* **Action Strategy:** Route future support tickets from this ID to automated macro workflows to preserve human engineering hours while monitoring margin drain.

### Case 5: Dormant High-Value Customer (Imminent Expiry)
* **Customer ID:** VIP-1052

### Case 6: Frequent Buyer Total Support Blackout

### Case 8: Exploding Complaint Spike

* **Risk Profile:** New customer experiencing severe onboarding or delivery friction. 7 tickets across only 4 order events indicates a broken first-mile brand experience.

### Case 9: High-Value Cart Abandonment Pattern

### Case 10: Chronic Low-Value Returns Profiler
* **Customer ID:** RET-1209
* **Metrics:** Recency: 8 days, Frequency: 15 orders, Monetary: $30, Complaints: 11 tickets
