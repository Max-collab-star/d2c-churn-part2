\# Strategic Retention and Budget Allocation Report



\## 1. Segment Distribution Metrics

Based on the execution of the heuristic RFM engine, the 2,400 unique customer records have been classified into five distinct operational tiers:



\* \*\*Loyal Customers:\*\* 1,001 users (41.71%)

\* \*\*Champions:\*\* 592 users (24.67%)

\* \*\*At-Risk Customers:\*\* 421 users (17.54%)

\* \*\*Dormant Customers:\*\* 276 users (11.50%)

\* \*\*High-Value But Unhappy:\*\* 110 users (4.58%)



\## 2. ROI-Driven Budget Allocation Strategy

With a strictly limited retention budget, funding must be directed where it prevents the most revenue drop-off per dollar spent, rather than sending blanket discounts.



| Target Segment | Budget Allocation | Actionable Tactical Campaign | Core Strategic Rationalization |

| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |

| \*\*High-Value But Unhappy\*\* | 45% of Budget | Direct 1-on-1 customer support outreach, immediate resolution of ticket bottlenecks, and high-priority shipping updates. | These 110 customers are our highest spenders but have high complaint rates ($\\ge 3$). Saving one customer here saves massive baseline revenue compared to any other tier. |

| \*\*At-Risk Customers\*\* | 35% of Budget | Time-sensitive re-engagement emails offering personalized product recommendations with a moderate 15% discount code. | These 421 users have low order frequency but bought recently. A small incentive can prevent them from sliding into the dormant pool. |

| \*\*Champions\*\* | 10% of Budget | Early access to new personal-care product releases and enrollment into an exclusive VIP milestone tier without direct discounts. | These 592 users are highly active and loyal. Sending them discounts drains margin unnecessarily because they already buy organically. |

| \*\*Loyal Customers\*\* | 10% of Budget | Standard milestone thank-you emails and points-based loyalty program upgrade invitations. | This is our largest bucket (1,001 users). They have steady baseline habits, so we focus on low-cost engagement over margin-eroding promotions. |

| \*\*Dormant Customers\*\* | 0% of Budget | Zero active budget allocation. Keep on a low-cost automated email newsletter sequence. | These 276 users haven't purchased in over 180 days. Win-back costs are extremely high with very low conversion success, making them a poor investment for a limited budget. |



\## 3. Measurable Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

To monitor the success of these targeted retention efforts, the team will track three primary operational metrics over the next 60 days:



1\. \*\*Segment Churn Rate:\*\* Specifically measuring if the churn percentage of the 110 "High-Value But Unhappy" users drops significantly below baseline levels following support interventions.

2\. \*\*Campaign Redemptions:\*\* Tracking the exact conversion rate of the 15% discount codes utilized by the "At-Risk" cohort.

3\. \*\*Customer Retention Cost (CRC):\*\* Monitoring the total marketing and support capital spent per retained customer to ensure ROI remains highly profitable.
