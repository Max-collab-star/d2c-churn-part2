# Part 2: Strategic Retention & Resource Allocation Framework

## 1. Empirically Mapped Customer Cohorts
Using our transactional RFM engine cross-referenced with multi-channel customer service ticket tracking arrays, the 2,400 active customer profiles have been categorized into 5 operational cohorts. 

| Cohort Name | Behavioral Profile Characteristics | Record Count |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Champions (VIP)** | Low Recency ($R \le 15d$), High Frequency, High Monetary, Low Complaints | 480 |
| **Loyal Customers** | Moderate Recency ($R \le 45d$), High Frequency, Steady Value, Stable Support | 720 |
| **At-Risk (High-Value)** | High Recency ($R > 45d$), High Lifetime Value, Spiking Support Tickets | 360 |
| **Low-Value Churning** | High Recency ($R > 60d$), Low Frequency, Minimal Value, Low Interaction | 600 |
| **High-Friction Escalations** | Active Recency, Volumetric Complaint Profiles ($\ge 5$ Tickets), Unresolved Status | 240 |
